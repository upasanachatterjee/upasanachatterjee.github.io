# Hi, I'm Upasana 👋

<p class="tagline">Computer science researcher · M.S. student at Columbia University · applying to PhD programs</p>

I study how we evaluate language models — and how to do it in ways that go
beyond outcome-based metrics to examine reasoning processes and internal
representations. By systematically identifying risks and failure modes, I hope
to advance our understanding of model behavior and help build AI systems that
are more interpretable, aligned, and safe to deploy.

## Research {#research}

<div class="entry" markdown="1">
<div class="entry-header"><strong>Causal AI Lab, Columbia University</strong> <span class="entry-dates">May 2026 – Present</span></div>

Studying factuality in LLMs from a causal perspective with Arvind Raghavan,
under Prof. Elias Bareinboim — applying causal discovery to link linguistic
features to entailment performance and characterize models' factual reasoning
errors.
</div>

<div class="entry" markdown="1">
<div class="entry-header"><strong>Mobile Social Lab, Columbia University</strong> <span class="entry-dates">Jan 2026 – Present</span></div>

Researching representation learning for auctions with Filipp Shelobolin, under
Prof. Augustin Chaintreau — investigating embedding-based auction
representations for predicting bidder behavior and outcomes.
</div>

<div class="entry" markdown="1">
<div class="entry-header"><strong>M.S. Thesis</strong> <span class="entry-dates">Jan 2025 – May 2026</span></div>

Developed a pre-training objective that leads LLMs to learn metalinguistic
features (e.g., theme, tone) predictive of political ideology in news. Used
causal mediation analysis to show that LLMs rely more on theme and tone than
human annotators do — and that fine-tuning does not reliably close the gap.
</div>
